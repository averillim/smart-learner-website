IGCSE

Biology

The scientific study of life. Themes covered in this course include scientific skills, ecology, biochemistry, cellular processes, classification of organisms and genetics.

Chemistry

An introduction to general, organic, and biological chemistry. Medical and health-related applications will be emphasised in this course.

Computer Science

The basic principles of computing. This course teaches computational thinking, programming, and problem-solving using computers.

English

The tools for clear and effective communication. This course focuses on speaking and writing skills like grammar, spelling and punctuation.

Geography

The basic concepts and processes that shape human geography. Global and local patterns of population, migration, environmental resources, agriculture, economy and urbanization are surveyed and the factors influencing these patterns are discussed.

History

Important chapters of the human past from the earliest civilizations through the 15th century. The course considers how people, environment, social movements, religion, political ideologies, and philosophical ideas have shaped human society.

Physics

Major concepts, methods, and applications of physics. Topics include a description of motion, Newton's Laws, conservation principles (energy and momentum), waves, thermodynamics, electricity, magnetism, optics, and modern physics.

World Literature

A survey of some of the major works of literature across the world from 1650 to the modern world.

SPM

Accounting

This course involves recording, analysing and reporting financial transactions in order for companies to be able to make strategic business decisions.

Business

This programme provides you with a comprehensive understanding of the main areas of business in order to prepare you for a career in management.

English

This course emphasizes the fundamental language skills of reading, writing, speaking, listening and presenting.

Geography

The study of the diverse environments, places, and spaces of Earth's surface and their interactions.

History

The discovery, collection, organisation and presentation of information about past events and important conflicts.

Islamic Studies

This course introduces core ideas and events which have shaped the Islamic world. Topics include the Qur'an and the development of Sharia law.

Malay

This course is designed to equip students with the necessary skills to use Bahasa Malaysia effectively for social, occupational and professional purposes.

Science

A universal subject that examines the structure and behavior of the physical and natural world through observation and experiment.
